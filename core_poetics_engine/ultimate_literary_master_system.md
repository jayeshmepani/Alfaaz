**Meaning Relationships:**

- **Synonyms:** Same/similar meaning ‚Äî _big / large_, _happy / joyful_
- **Antonyms:** Opposite meaning, with subtypes:
  - **Gradable:** _hot / cold_ (spectrum)

- **Auto-antonyms / Contronyms:** A word meaning its own opposite ‚Äî _sanction_ (permit / penalize), _dust_ (remove / add dust), _oversight_ (supervision / failure to notice)

**Form-Based Relationships:**

- **Paronyms:** Words that look/sound similar but differ in meaning ‚Äî _affect / effect_, _historic / historical_, _beside / besides_
- **Capitonyms:** Meaning changes when capitalized ‚Äî _turkey_ (bird) / _Turkey_ (country), _polish_ / _Polish_

**Sound & Structure Relationships:**

- **Minimal Pairs:** Words differing by only one sound ‚Äî _bat / pat_, _ship / sheep_ (critical for phonological analysis and dialect assessment)

- **Malapropisms:** Wrong word used that sounds similar to the intended one ‚Äî "illiterate him from your memory" (instead of _obliterate_) ‚Äî flag as error unless deliberate characterization
- **Anagrams:** Same letters rearranged ‚Äî _listen / silent_, _evil / vile_ (sometimes used for symbolic encoding in poetry)

**2.4 Qualitative Analysis Pipeline (TP-CASTT):**

1.  **T**itle ‚Äî Before reading, hypothesize what the poem is about from the title alone
2.  **P**araphrase ‚Äî Translate line-by-line into literal meaning
3.  **C**onnotation ‚Äî Identify figurative devices, metaphors, symbolism, imagery

**3.4 Critical Evaluation Lenses:**

- **Objective Correlative (T. S. Eliot):** Does the poem supply a concrete chain of objects, situations, or imagery that reliably evokes the intended emotion _without naming it_? If the reader must be told what to feel rather than experiencing it through the imagery, the correlative has failed.
